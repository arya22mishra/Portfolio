About

Why this
work matters

"Real change happens when we stop designing for communities and start designing with them."

I came into education through Teach for India — placed in a low-income school in Bengaluru with the belief that great teaching could change a child's trajectory. Two years and 52 students later, I understood something deeper: the most powerful moments happened when I stepped back and let students lead.

That conviction carries into my work at Involve Learning Solution Foundation, where I lead solution design. I build structures that make student voice the operating system of how schools function. The Bal Sabha model I'm designing across 150+ government schools is rooted in this belief.

The gram sabha pilot changed how I think about program design. Watching Panchayat members shift from skepticism to partnership — because students showed up with real evidence — made clear that evidence is a form of power.

Whether it's a peer-teaching matrix, a CoP workbook, or a session on innovative thinking — the question I always return to is: does this give more agency to the people it touches?
